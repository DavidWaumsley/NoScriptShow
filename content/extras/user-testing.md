# Simple User Testing Checkist

This checklist covers the essential steps for preparing and running a live (moderated) user testing session, ensuring you remain neutral and capture actionable insights.

### Preparation (Before the Session)

- **Define 1–3 clear goals:** Identify exactly what you want to learn (e.g. "Can users find X information?").
- **Recruit 3-5 participants** who match your target audience.
- **Write scenario-based tasks:** Instead of "Click the contact page," use a scenario: "You need to ask a question about your order; show me how you'd get in touch".

- **Prepare your tech:** Test your Zoom link, screen-sharing settings, and recording software.
-  **Conduct a pilot test:** Run a "dress rehearsal" with a colleague to catch confusing wording or broken prototype links.

### Introduction (First 5 Minutes)

- **Set the stage:** Explain that you are testing the website, not the person. If they get stuck, it is a flaw in the design, not them.
- **Obtain consent:** Verbally confirm they are okay with being recorded for internal research purposes.
- **Explain "Think Aloud":** Ask them to narrate their thoughts, frustrations, and expectations as they navigate.

### 3. During the Test (The Tasks)

- **Provide one task at a time:** Give the user a written version (via chat) so they can refer back to it.
- **Stay neutral:** Avoid "helping" the user. If they ask a question, respond with: "What would you do if I wasn't here?".
- **Prompt for silence:** If they stop talking for more than 15 seconds, gently ask: "What are you thinking right now?".
- **Watch for body language:** Note where they squint, hesitate, or click the "back" button repeatedly.

### 4. Wrap-Up & Analysis
- **Ask follow-up questions:** "What was the most frustrating part of that process?" or "What did you expect to happen when you clicked that?".
- **Thank and compensate:** Confirm how they will receive their incentive (e.g., gift card) and end the recording.
- **Synthesize immediately:** Take 10 minutes right after the session to note the top 3 issues you observed while they are fresh in your mind.
